<p><p>The wine closures market consists of three primary types: cork, screwcap, and plastic. Cork is traditional, providing natural breathability and aging potential, but it risks cork taint. Screwcap offers consistent sealing and better protection against oxidation, gaining popularity for its convenience and reliability. Plastic closures, often used for lower-end wines, are lightweight and cost-effective but may lack the integrity of cork and screwcap options. Each type serves distinct consumer preferences and wine styles, influencing their market dynamics.</p></p>
