<p><p>Automatic hematocrit centrifuges are categorized into two primary types: desktop and floor-standing models. Desktop centrifuges are compact, designed for smaller laboratories or clinics with limited space, offering portability and ease of use. They typically handle smaller sample volumes, making them ideal for routine tests. In contrast, floor-standing centrifuges are larger, designed for high-throughput labs, capable of processing multiple samples simultaneously.
