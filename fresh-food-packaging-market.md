<p><p>The fresh food packaging market comprises two primary types: rigid and flexible packaging. Rigid packaging includes materials like glass, plastic, and metal, offering durability and protection for perishable items. It is ideal for items requiring structural integrity, such as jars or containers. Flexible packaging, on the other hand, utilizes materials like films and pouches, allowing for lightweight, space-saving options that can conform to product shapes. This type is often used for items like snacks and deli products, enhancing convenience and shelf life.</p></p>
