<p><p>The lithium-ion battery market primarily features two types of electrolytes: liquid and solid. Liquid electrolytes, traditionally used, are composed of lithium salts dissolved in organic solvents, providing high ionic conductivity but posing safety risks like leakage and flammability. In contrast, solid electrolytes, an emerging technology, offer enhanced safety and thermal stability while improving energy density and cycle life. These solid solutions replace liquid components, potentially overcoming the limitations of liquid electrolytes and enabling advancements in battery performance and application.</p></p>
