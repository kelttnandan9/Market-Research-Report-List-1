<p><p>The electrical insulated gloves market is categorized into two primary types: low voltage and high voltage gloves. Low voltage gloves are designed for tasks involving electrical systems up to 1,000 volts, providing essential protection for workers handling low-power equipment and wiring. On the other hand, high voltage gloves are engineered for higher electrical hazards, safeguarding users from voltages exceeding 1,000 volts, commonly used in maintenance and repair of high-powered electrical systems. Both types ensure safety against electrical shock and arc flash incidents.</p></p>
