<p><p>The arthroscopic instruments market is divided into two primary types: handheld and benchtop instruments. Handheld instruments are compact, portable devices used by surgeons during minimally invasive procedures, allowing for precision and control. In contrast, benchtop instruments are larger, stationary devices that support various surgical functions, often providing advanced capabilities for diagnostics and treatments. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing surgical outcomes, with handheld tools emphasizing mobility and precision, while benchtop instruments focus on functionality and support in a clinical setting.</p></p>
