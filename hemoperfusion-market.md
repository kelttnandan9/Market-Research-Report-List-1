<p><ul><li>Activated Carbon Adsorption</li><li>Resin Adsorption</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The hemoperfusion market features two primary types: activated carbon adsorption and resin adsorption. Activated carbon adsorption utilizes carbon's high surface area to capture toxins and metabolic waste from blood, making it effective for various poisoning cases. In contrast, resin adsorption employs synthetic resins that selectively bind specific toxins or drugs, offering a targeted approach to treatment. Both methods aim to purify blood, reduce toxic load, and enhance therapeutic outcomes in patients with renal failure or overdose situations.</p></p>
