<p><p>Evaporation coating machines primarily focus on two methods: Physical Vapor Deposition (PVD) and Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD). PVD utilizes physical processes, like sputtering or thermal evaporation, to deposit thin coatings, often enhancing hardness and wear resistance. In contrast, CVD employs chemical reactions to form films on substrates, effective for complex shapes and providing uniform coatings. Both techniques are crucial in industries like electronics, optics, and automotive, offering tailored surfaces for diverse applications, including decorative and functional purposes.</p></p>
